# Zaria Forman Documents Climate Change in Her Pastel Drawings: Environmental Art Commentary

Zaria Forman has developed significant artistic practice creating large-scale pastel drawings documenting environmental changes, climate impacts, and threatened landscapes. The artworks employ detailed realistic rendering combined with conscious aesthetic sophistication, creating images possessing both documentary quality and fine art status. The subjects include threatened glaciers, flooding environments, climate-affected landscapes, and human communities facing environmental challenges. The monumental scale of the drawings—often exceeding human height—emphasizes the importance of subject matter and creates immersive viewing experiences. The technique combines pastel's distinctive visual properties with meticulous detail work enabling documentation of environmental conditions with clarity and emotional resonance. The work appeals simultaneously to audiences interested in environmental concerns, fine art, landscape depiction, and climate communication. The artistic approach differs from conventional environmental activism or scientific documentation, instead employing traditional visual art media to address environmental urgency.

The technical execution demonstrates mastery of pastel medium combined with ambitious large-scale work. Pastel's distinctive properties—the saturated colors, the capacity for blending, the visual texture created by pigment on paper—contribute to the artworks' aesthetic impact. The detailed realism requires extraordinary precision working at large scale with medium that can be difficult to control at monumental dimensions. The artist must understand how light affects these environments—the glacial blues, the atmospheric effects, the luminosity of endangered landscapes—and translate these qualities through pastel. The permanence of pastel is limited; the artworks must be carefully housed and protected, emphasizing their preciousness and vulnerability. The sophistication of technical execution rivals landscape painters working in other media while employing pastel's distinctive aesthetic properties.

The conceptual framework engages with environmental urgency, climate change documentation, and art's potential to communicate climate concerns. Rather than employing conventional scientific visualization or activist rhetoric, Forman employs traditional fine art media to address environmental urgency. The large-scale realistic renderings create emotional impact through aesthetic engagement; viewers encounter beautiful but threatened landscapes documented with clarity and meticulous detail. The realism emphasizes that these environmental conditions represent actual circumstances rather than speculation or projection. The monumentality of the artworks reflects the significance of environmental issues; the scale suggests that these concerns merit serious artistic attention comparable to historical subjects commanding monumental treatment. The choice of endangered landscapes as subject matter asserts that environmental documentation belongs within fine art discourse alongside other legitimate artistic subjects.

The range across Forman's practice demonstrates diverse environmental subjects while maintaining consistent commitment to documentation through realistic large-scale pastel drawing. Some works focus specifically on glacial environments; others address flooding, environmental degradation, or climate impacts on human communities. Some employ aerial perspectives; others document ground-level environmental conditions. The diversity demonstrates that environmental urgency manifests across diverse geographic and environmental contexts. Each artwork succeeds or fails based on its technical execution, emotional resonance, and effectiveness in communicating environmental conditions. The consistency of excellence across varied approaches suggests genuine commitment to environmental documentation combined with mastery of pastel medium.

Forman's environmental art practice exemplifies how artists can engage meaningfully with climate change and environmental urgency through fine art practices. The work demonstrates that environmental communication need not rely on scientific visualization or explicit activism; instead, traditional aesthetic and formal concerns can address environmental urgency effectively. The emotional power of the artworks derives from aesthetic sophistication combined with subject matter significance. For artists interested in environmental themes, contemporary social concerns, or combining fine art traditions with urgent subject matter, Forman's work serves as inspiration demonstrating how traditional media can address contemporary environmental crises. Through large-scale pastel drawings documenting climate change, Zaria Forman has created practice demonstrating art's potential to communicate environmental urgency while maintaining aesthetic and conceptual sophistication.
