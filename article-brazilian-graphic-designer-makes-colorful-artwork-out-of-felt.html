Brazilian Graphic Designer Makes Colorful Artwork Out of Felt

By Amanda C - August 12, 2019

A Brazilian graphic designer has created distinctive and visually striking artworks using felt as a primary medium, crafting colorful, textured compositions that celebrate the visual and tactile qualities of felt as an artistic material. The designer's approach to felt-based artwork emphasizes color relationships and compositional dynamics, with the inherent qualities of felt—its soft surface, matte finish, and varied color possibilities—used to maximum aesthetic effect. The artworks range from abstract compositions exploring color and form to representational work featuring recognizable subjects rendered in felt. The process of creating these felt artworks involves careful cutting, layering, and arrangement of felt pieces, creating dimensional works that exist somewhere between painting and sculpture.

The color palettes employed are often vibrant and unexpected, with combinations that demonstrate sophisticated color sense and understanding of color relationships. The surface quality of the felt contributes significantly to the visual character of the work, with the soft, matte finish creating a visual and tactile experience distinct from conventional painting or drawing media. The work reflects Brazilian visual culture and aesthetic sensibilities, with color choices and compositional approaches influenced by the designer's cultural context. The dimensionality and tactile quality of the work makes it particularly engaging in person, with viewers drawn to the textural and dimensional qualities that photograph and reproduction cannot fully capture.

The work has been exhibited in galleries internationally and has gained particular recognition for the innovative approach to felt as an artistic medium. Through creating colorful artwork from felt, this Brazilian designer has expanded the possibilities of artistic practice while celebrating the aesthetic and material qualities of felt.
