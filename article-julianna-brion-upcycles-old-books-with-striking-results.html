Julianna Brion Upcycles Old Books With Striking Results

By Amanda C - August 22, 2019

Julianna Brion has developed a distinctive artistic practice involving the upcycling and transformation of old books into striking and visually compelling artworks through creative cutting, folding, and arrangement of pages and bindings. Her practice represents a form of artistic salvage and creative reuse, transforming materials that might otherwise be discarded into new artworks that celebrate the visual and tactile qualities of books as physical objects. The process typically involves careful deconstruction of books, selective retention of pages or bindings, and recombination of these elements into new sculptural or pictorial compositions. The resulting artworks are often visually striking, with the layered pages and arranged bindings creating dimensional forms that play with light and shadow.

The text and imagery from the source books frequently remain visible within the artworks, creating layers of meaning and conceptual resonance. Brion's practice engages with themes of memory, history, and the preservation of knowledge, with the transformation of old books suggesting the remaking and reinterpretation of existing cultural material. The work raises questions about obsolescence, value, and the relationship between preservation and destruction. The visual documentation of these book artworks in exhibitions often emphasizes the dimensional and sculptural qualities that become apparent in person.

The sustainability dimension of her practice resonates particularly strongly with contemporary audiences concerned about consumption and environmental impact. Her work has been exhibited internationally and has achieved recognition for the ingenuity and artistic vision applied to found and recycled materials. Through upcycling old books with striking results, Julianna Brion has created artwork that celebrates creative reuse while engaging with meaningful conceptual themes.
