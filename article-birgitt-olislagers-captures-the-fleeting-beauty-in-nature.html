Puppet making represents artistic practice creating sculptural objects designed for performance and animation, bridging sculpture, theater, and performance art. Contemporary puppet artists create objects ranging from simple hand puppets to complex marionettes incorporating mechanical sophistication and elaborate costuming. Puppet art encompasses traditional shadow puppetry, elaborate Japanese bunraku theater traditions, and contemporary experimental approaches incorporating puppetry with multimedia performance. The practice demonstrates that sculptural objects can function simultaneously as aesthetic objects and performance implements, generating rich artistic traditions combining visual art and theatrical spectacle.
