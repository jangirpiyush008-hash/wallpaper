This Artist Creates Chocolates in the Shape of Everyday Objects

By Bharti J - July 9, 2019

An artist has developed an innovative and whimsical practice of creating elaborate chocolates and confections shaped and styled to resemble everyday objects, transforming recognizable items from daily life into edible art objects. The chocolates are meticulously crafted, with exceptional attention to detail and surface treatment that makes them visually convincing representations of the objects they mimic. The selection of everyday objects as subjects creates an interesting juxtaposition between the mundane and the precious, transforming humble subjects into luxury goods through the application of chocolate. The technical skill required to create these chocolate objects is considerable, involving careful mold creation, precise temperature control, and detailed hand-finishing work.

The visual documentation of these chocolate objects often emphasizes the fine details and convincingness of the chocolate representations. The practice raises interesting questions about the relationship between appearance and actuality, with viewers drawn to the cognitive dissonance of objects that appear to be everyday items but are actually edible chocolate creations. The work celebrates the possibilities for skilled craftsmanship to transform familiar materials into surprising and delightful objects. Through chocolates shaped like everyday objects, this artist has created work that celebrates craftsmanship, surprise, and the delight of discovering that precious materials hide within humble appearances.
