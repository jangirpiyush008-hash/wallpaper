Knitters Around The World Are Knitting Sweaters For Sign-Posts

By Chris L - August 20, 2019

A global movement of knitters has emerged around the practice of knitting sweaters and other garments specifically for sign-posts, street furniture, and urban infrastructure elements, transforming these functional objects into unexpected canvases for colorful textile art. This practice, sometimes referred to as yarn bombing or textile graffiti, involves creating knitted pieces designed to fit specific architectural or infrastructure elements within urban environments, then installing these pieces with the intention of temporarily or permanently altering the visual character of public spaces. The sweaters, hats, and other garments knitted for sign-posts are often vibrantly colored and intricately patterned, creating striking visual interventions that draw attention to otherwise unremarkable features of the urban landscape. The practice involves careful attention to measurement and fitting, as the knitted pieces must be sized appropriately for the specific posts or features they're intended to cover.

The knitters involved frequently work collaboratively, with different individuals contributing pieces to larger installations or coordinating timing and locations for simultaneous installations across multiple cities. The practice engages questions about public space, artistic expression, property rights, and the relationship between permanent and temporary interventions in the built environment. The knitted works are often created from recycled or reclaimed materials, adding an environmental dimension to the practice and demonstrating creative resourcefulness. The practice has achieved significant visibility through social media documentation and has inspired knitters internationally to engage in their own yarn bombing projects.

The practice celebrates traditional textile skills and the visual possibilities of knitting while applying these skills to contemporary questions about public space and artistic intervention. Through knitting sweaters for sign-posts, this global community of knitters has created an accessible form of artistic expression that transforms urban landscapes and brings color and creativity to public spaces.
