Kip Omolade Creates Hyperrealistic Portraits and Chrome Face Masks

By Dan J - August 27, 2019

Kip Omolade is a talented artist known for creating stunning hyperrealistic portraits that demonstrate extraordinary technical skill and sophisticated understanding of likeness and portraiture, alongside experimental work exploring the intersection of the human face and digital or material manipulation. His hyperrealistic portraits are rendered with meticulous attention to detail, capturing not just likeness but also subtle qualities of expression and personality that make the works feel alive and psychologically present. Beyond traditional portraiture, Omolade has developed a distinctive practice involving the creation of chrome face masks and reflective facial sculptures that distort and fragment the human face while simultaneously celebrating its formal properties and geometric qualities. These reflective and chrome works explore the intersection of the human form and technological aesthetics, questioning ideas about identity, reflection, and the relationship between appearance and authentic self.

The technical skill required to create these works is extraordinary, involving mastery of both traditional portraiture techniques and contemporary sculptural and digital processes. His hyperrealistic portraits often incorporate elements of surprise or unexpected juxtaposition, such as portions of the face rendered in chrome or reflective material or unexpected compositional choices that challenge conventional portraiture approaches. Omolade's work engages with contemporary concerns about identity formation, the mediation of self-presentation through digital technologies, and the philosophical questions raised by tools that allow manipulation and alteration of facial appearance. His works have been exhibited internationally in galleries and museums, with particular recognition for the technical virtuosity and conceptual sophistication of his practice.

Through his hyperrealistic portraits and experimental face masks, Kip Omolade has created work that celebrates the human face while questioning and reimagining its representation and meaning.
