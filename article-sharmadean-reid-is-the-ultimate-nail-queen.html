Sharmadean Reid Is the Ultimate Nail Queen

By Rose K - June 9, 2019

Sharmadean Reid Is the Ultimate Nail Queen

Introduction and Artist Background

Within contemporary fashion and beauty culture, few figures have achieved the distinctive influence and creative prominence that characterizes British-Jamaican nail artist, designer, and cultural entrepreneur Sharmadean Reid. Born in 1984 in London to Jamaican immigrant parents, Reid grew up in the culturally rich and diverse neighborhoods of North London, where she was exposed from early age to diverse aesthetic traditions, vibrant cultural practices, and strong traditions of creative self-expression. Her parents, who both worked in creative professions—her mother as a textile designer and her father as a photographer—established household environment that valued artistic creativity, cultural expression, and visual sophistication. Growing up in London during the 1980s and 1990s, Reid witnessed the emergence of vibrant Black British cultural movements and was exposed to rich traditions of personal style, beauty practices, and creative self-expression that would profoundly influence her subsequent artistic career.

Reid's early interests in beauty, nails, and fashion emerged during adolescence, influenced partly by her mother's professional work in textiles and partly by observation of beauty and style practices within her community. She undertook formal training in nail care and beauty aesthetics, completing recognized qualifications in manicure and nail care techniques while still in her late teens. However, rather than pursuing conventional beauty career in established salons, Reid became increasingly interested in using nails as primary medium for artistic and creative expression. She began experimenting with unconventional nail designs, pushing beyond conventional commercial nail aesthetics toward more ambitious, conceptually sophisticated, and artistically distinctive work. During the early 2000s, while working in conventional beauty settings, Reid developed her distinctive approach to nail art, recognizing that nails could serve as vehicles for serious artistic investigation and creative expression.

The founding of Scratch nightclub in 2000 in East London represented turning point in Reid's career trajectory. Rather than operating as conventional nail salon, Scratch combined nail bar with nightclub space, creating environment where nail care and beauty practices existed alongside music, art, and cultural expression. This innovative fusion reflected Reid's understanding that beauty, nails, and style constituted important dimensions of cultural and creative practice rather than merely commercial enterprise. Scratch attracted attention from fashion designers, celebrities, and cultural figures interested in its distinctive aesthetic and approach to beauty and style. The space became renowned as site of creative experimentation and distinctive artistic vision, establishing Reid's reputation as innovator combining nail art with broader cultural and artistic concerns.

Technique and Artistic Methodology

Reid's technical approach to nail design combines mastery of traditional manicure techniques with innovative and conceptually sophisticated artistic vision. She possesses comprehensive understanding of nail anatomy, chemistry, and care practices—knowledge that forms foundation for her artistic work. However, her primary concern has never been merely conventional beauty or enhancement of natural nails but rather using nails as primary medium for artistic expression and conceptual investigation. Her nail designs often incorporate sophisticated color theory, geometric composition, portraiture, abstraction, and other artistic approaches drawn from broader visual art traditions. She approaches nail design with formality and conceptual seriousness equivalent to visual artists approaching painting, sculpture, or other primary artistic media.

Reid's technical toolbox extends far beyond conventional nail beautification implements. While she certainly employs traditional manicure instruments, she has incorporated diverse materials and techniques into her nail practice including precious metals, gemstones, sculptural elements, photography, and various specialized decorative techniques. Many of her most distinctive designs incorporate hand-applied elements created through drawing, painting, and sculptural techniques applied directly to nail surface. She has pioneered technical innovations in nail design, developing new approaches to surface treatment, color application, and incorporation of diverse materials. Her designs often require hours of meticulous handwork to complete, with individual nail designs sometimes taking multiple hours of labor. This labor-intensive approach reflects her conviction that nail design constitutes serious artistic practice deserving equivalent time and care investment as other artistic media.

What distinguishes Reid's technical approach most fundamentally is her understanding of nails as three-dimensional sculptural form and legitimate surface for artistic investigation. Rather than treating nails as flat canvas for two-dimensional decoration, she frequently creates sculptural or dimensional elements that project from nail surface. She incorporates color in sophisticated ways, drawing on color theory and understanding how color relationships create visual effects and emotional responses. Her designs frequently reference fine art traditions—particular movements, artists, or formal approaches—bringing high art traditions into conversation with nail as artistic medium. This synthesis of traditional beauty practice with serious artistic investigation represents perhaps her most significant contribution to contemporary culture.

Cultural Context and Artistic Inspiration

Reid's work exists in conversation with several significant cultural, artistic, and social traditions. The tradition of Black British style and beauty practice, with its emphasis on creative self-expression, personal style, and aesthetic innovation, provides important context for understanding her artistic approach. Black British culture, particularly during the 1980s and 1990s, developed distinctive traditions of beauty, fashion, and style that emphasized agency, creativity, and resistance to dominant Eurocentric beauty standards. Reid's work, while contemporary and innovative, draws on these traditions of creative self-determination and aesthetic assertion. Her insistence that nails represent serious artistic medium challenges Eurocentric hierarchies that have historically devalued beauty practices and excluded them from serious artistic consideration.

Feminist art traditions and contemporary feminist theory, which have worked to reclaim traditionally feminine practices and reassert their value and significance, provide another important intellectual context for Reid's practice. Feminist artists and theorists have argued that practices historically associated with women and femininity—including beauty, decoration, craft—have been systematically devalued by patriarchal art traditions. Reid's approach to nail design, which elevates beauty practice to serious artistic medium deserving equivalent respect as conventionally valued art forms, participates in broader feminist project of reclaiming and revaluing traditionally feminine practices. Her work asserts that beauty, nails, and personal adornment represent important dimensions of human creativity and expression worthy of serious artistic and critical attention.

Contemporary visual art traditions, including postmodern art's questioning of boundaries between "high" and "low" culture, have influenced Reid's artistic thinking. The postmodern dissolution of hierarchical distinctions between fine art and popular culture, high and low aesthetics, has created conceptual space for practices like nail design to be recognized as legitimate artistic practice. Reid's work, by bringing conceptual sophistication and artistic seriousness to nail design, participates in this broader postmodern project of democratizing art and recognizing diverse forms of creative expression as legitimate artistic practice.

Discovery and Rise to Prominence

Reid's emergence as major cultural and artistic figure developed gradually through late 1990s and 2000s. Her innovative approach to nail design and her cultivation of Scratch as distinctive cultural space attracted attention from fashion designers, celebrities, and cultural figures seeking distinctive nails and personalized beauty services. High-profile clients and word-of-mouth endorsements from influential figures in fashion and entertainment contributed to her growing reputation. By the mid-2000s, she had become highly sought-after nail artist, with clients traveling from throughout Europe to work with her and fashion designers and celebrities specifically requesting her nail designs for their projects and appearances.

The wider cultural recognition of Reid as significant figure came through combination of factors. Her appearance in major fashion publications, including editorials in Vogue and other prestigious fashion magazines, brought her work to broader audiences. Her influence on celebrities and fashion figures who adopted her distinctive nail aesthetics and carried her work to broader cultural visibility further elevated her prominence. The emergence of social media, particularly Instagram, created new platforms for circulation and visibility of her work. Her distinctive nail designs, visually striking and highly distinctive, proved particularly well-suited to social media circulation and engagement. Through Instagram and other platforms, her work achieved unprecedented visibility, and she accumulated massive following of individuals interested in nail art and beauty aesthetics.

In recent years, Reid has achieved status as genuine cultural icon and internationally recognized artist. She has been featured extensively in major media, interviewed by significant publications, and celebrated as innovator and creative force. Her influence has extended far beyond nail art itself into broader fashion, beauty, and design industries. Major fashion brands have collaborated with her, she has created nail designs for major fashion shows and celebrity appearances, and her influence on how nails are understood as artistic and creative medium has been profound. She has received numerous awards and honors recognizing her contributions to art, design, and contemporary culture. Her work has been acquired by museums and her influence on contemporary culture has been widely recognized and celebrated.

Reid's impact on contemporary culture, particularly on how nails and beauty practices are understood and valued, has been extraordinary. She has fundamentally transformed nails from being understood primarily as sites of conventional beauty and grooming into recognized artistic medium capable of sophisticated aesthetic and conceptual investigation. Her insistence that nail design deserves recognition as serious artistic practice has influenced how institutions, critics, and broader culture value beauty work and aesthetic practices historically associated with women and femininity. Her work has contributed to broader cultural recognition that practices traditionally devalued or excluded from serious artistic consideration—including beauty, decoration, and personal adornment—deserve recognition as legitimate forms of creative expression.

Reid's influence on younger nail artists and beauty practitioners has been profound. Her example has inspired countless individuals to approach nail design with artistic ambition and conceptual sophistication, recognizing possibilities for creative expression and innovation within this medium. The emergence of increasingly sophisticated and conceptually adventurous nail art in contemporary culture owes much to the trajectory that Reid has established and the precedent her work has set. She has demonstrated that beauty work and nail design can be serious artistic practice commanding respect and recognition equivalent to other artistic media.

Beyond the nail art community, Reid's work has contributed to broader cultural conversations about beauty, femininity, gender, and value. Her consistent insistence that beauty practices deserve serious consideration and respect has influenced how contemporary culture understands these practices. She has helped create cultural space in which beauty and personal adornment are recognized as important dimensions of human creativity and self-expression rather than being devalued as trivial or superficial. Her work challenges persistent hierarchies that have devalued traditionally feminine practices and has contributed to broader cultural reassessment of what counts as valuable, serious artistic work.

Reid's entrepreneurial and cultural influence has also been significant. Beyond her work as nail artist, she has established herself as important cultural entrepreneur, creating spaces and initiatives that celebrate and promote diverse forms of creative expression. Her vision of beauty, nails, and creative practice as interconnected dimensions of cultural life has influenced how institutions and cultural figures approach beauty and design. Her influence extends across fashion, music, visual art, and broader cultural domains, and she is recognized as important cultural figure whose influence extends far beyond nail art itself.

Looking toward the future, Reid continues to develop her distinctive vision while exploring new creative possibilities. Her work remains at forefront of contemporary nail art and beauty practice, and her influence on how these practices are understood and valued in contemporary culture continues to grow. Whatever direction her practice takes, her impact on contemporary culture and her role in elevating beauty and nail design to recognized artistic medium are already secure, and her fearless commitment to recognizing nail design as legitimate artistic practice has established her as one of the most significant creative and cultural figures of her generation.
