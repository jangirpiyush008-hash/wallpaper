Fotini Tikkou’s Art is All About Self-Acceptance

By Amanda C - August 25, 2019

Fotini Tikkou is an artist whose work centers on themes of self-acceptance, body positivity, and the celebration of human diversity, creating artworks that challenge conventional beauty standards and promote inclusive representations of the human form. Her practice encompasses various media including painting, drawing, and mixed media, all unified by a commitment to depicting bodies and identities in ways that celebrate difference and reject narrow standards of beauty and acceptability. Tikkou's approach to figuration emphasizes the unique characteristics and distinctive features of individual bodies, creating portraits and figurative works that celebrate rather than minimize these distinguishing features. Her color palettes are often warm and flattering, with skin tones rendered with sophistication and sensitivity that honors the diverse range of human complexion without resorting to stereotypical or flattening approaches.

The compositions of her works often position subjects with dignity and confidence, inviting viewers to engage with her subjects as fully human and worthy of respect and admiration. Her work frequently incorporates text and symbolic elements that reinforce messages of self-acceptance and body positivity, creating artworks that function simultaneously as visual and verbal statements. The representation of different body types, ages, abilities, and identities in her work contributes to a broader cultural conversation about who is considered worthy of artistic attention and celebration. Her work has been exhibited internationally and has achieved particular recognition in communities advocating for body positivity and inclusive representation in visual culture.

Through her art celebrating self-acceptance, Fotini Tikkou has created a body of work that challenges viewers to reconsider their own internalized beauty standards and to value human diversity.
