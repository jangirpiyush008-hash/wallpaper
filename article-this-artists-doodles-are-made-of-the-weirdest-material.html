This Artist’s Doodles Are Made of The Weirdest Material

By Ronnie H - August 19, 2019

An artist has developed a distinctly unconventional and surprising practice of creating doodles, drawings, and detailed artworks using materials that are decidedly unusual and unexpected for artistic practice. Rather than employing traditional drawing materials such as pencils, pens, or charcoal, this artist creates detailed artworks using materials ranging from food substances to industrial materials, creating works that challenge assumptions about what constitutes appropriate art media. The use of unconventional materials adds conceptual layers to the work, raising questions about artistic practice, the role of medium in art-making, and the relationship between material choice and meaning. The doodles created in these unusual media often demonstrate exceptional detail and control despite the inherent challenges posed by non-traditional materials.

The practice reflects an experimental and boundary-pushing approach to artistic creation, treating any material as a potential artistic medium if it allows for the creation of visual marks and forms. The visual documentation of these works in unconventional media often involves photography that captures details and nuances that might be lost in person or that emphasize the unusual material characteristics of the work. The artist's willingness to work with materials considered inappropriate or problematic for art-making contributes to broader conversations about artistic legitimacy, material choices, and the democratization of artistic practice. The works created in these unusual media often achieve viral attention through social media documentation, as viewers respond with surprise and delight to the ingenuity and skill demonstrated through working with unfamiliar and challenging materials.

Through creating doodles and artworks in the weirdest materials, this artist has challenged conventional assumptions about art-making and has expanded the possibilities of artistic practice.
