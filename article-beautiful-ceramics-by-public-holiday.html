Beautiful Ceramics by Public Holiday

By Chris L - August 25, 2019

Public Holiday is an artist and designer collective known for creating beautiful, functional ceramic objects that combine aesthetic sophistication with practical utility, resulting in pieces that elevate everyday domestic life. The collective's work spans a range of ceramic forms including tableware, vessels, sculptural objects, and architectural elements, all characterized by careful attention to form, surface treatment, and material qualities. Their approach to ceramics emphasizes the material itself, celebrating the unique qualities of clay and the firing process rather than attempting to disguise or transcend the material's inherent nature. Each piece demonstrates exceptional craftsmanship and technical skill, with glazes and surface treatments that are carefully developed and refined through extensive experimentation and testing.

The aesthetic language of Public Holiday's work is distinctive and recognizable, characterized by bold forms, sophisticated color palettes, and an approach to decoration that balances restraint with visual interest. Their ceramics frequently feature geometric and organic forms in dialogue with one another, creating visual complexity through the interplay of different formal approaches. The functional aspects of their ceramics are integral to the artistic conception, with forms designed to be visually beautiful while serving their intended purpose effectively and comfortably. Their work is exhibited in museums and galleries internationally, with pieces held in major public and private collections.

The collective has also worked on architectural commissions and large-scale installation projects, demonstrating the capacity of their design language to operate at different scales and contexts. Through their beautiful ceramics, Public Holiday has demonstrated that contemporary ceramic practice can simultaneously honor traditional craftsmanship and material knowledge while creating work that is relevant and compelling to contemporary audiences.
