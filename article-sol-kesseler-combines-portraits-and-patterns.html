Sol Kesseler Combines Portraits and Patterns

By Amanda C - August 28, 2019

Sol Kesseler's distinctive artistic practice combines portraiture with elaborate pattern work, creating visually complex compositions that explore the relationship between human identity and decorative expression. Her innovative approach involves layering intricate patterned backgrounds and elements with sensitive, carefully observed portrait work, resulting in images that are both intimate and visually overwhelming in their density and complexity. Kesseler's portraits are notable for their psychological depth and sensitivity to the individuality of her subjects, while the patterned elements introduce a layer of visual richness that complicates and enriches the viewing experience. The patterns she incorporates often relate thematically to the subjects portrayed, creating additional layers of meaning and personal connection beyond the portrait itself.

Her color choices are sophisticated and carefully considered, with palettes that enhance both the portrait and the decorative elements, creating visual harmony despite the work's inherent complexity. Kesseler's process often involves extensive research into her subjects, incorporating biographical and historical elements into the patterned backgrounds and decorative components of her work. This approach creates portraits that function simultaneously as representations of individual human beings and as complex visual and conceptual artworks. Her work has been exhibited internationally and has gained particular recognition for its innovative approach to contemporary portraiture.

The combination of portraiture and pattern-making in her work demonstrates a deep engagement with the history of both practices, from Renaissance portraiture to the decorative traditions of various cultures. Through this synthesis, Kesseler has created a distinctive visual language that celebrates both human individuality and the beauty of decorative expression, suggesting that these approaches need not be considered separately or hierarchically.
