Ana Jaks’ Art is a Homage to Shapes and Colors

By Dan J - July 31, 2019

Ana Jaks' artistic practice centers on the exploration of shapes and colors as primary subjects and means of artistic expression, creating works that celebrate the visual and formal possibilities of fundamental geometric and organic forms. Her approach to art-making emphasizes the intrinsic beauty and interest present in basic shapes and color relationships, creating artworks that operate primarily on formal and perceptual levels rather than depending on representation or narrative. The compositions she creates often feature geometric and organic forms arranged in dynamic relationships that create visual movement and engagement. The color palettes employed are often bold and saturated, with careful attention to color relationships and the optical effects created through specific chromatic combinations.

Her work demonstrates exceptional understanding of color theory and the ways in which colors interact optically to create visual effects and emotional resonances. The forms she employs range from pure geometric shapes to more organic, biomorphic forms that suggest natural processes and organic growth. The scale and scale relationships between forms create compositional dynamics that guide viewer attention and create visual hierarchy despite the absence of representational imagery. Her practice engages with abstract art traditions while creating contemporary work that speaks to contemporary contexts and concerns.

The work invites viewers into sustained visual engagement, rewarding careful attention and contemplation with increasingly sophisticated perceptual experiences. Through art that celebrates shapes and colors, Ana Jaks has created work that demonstrates the continued power and relevance of formal artistic concerns.
