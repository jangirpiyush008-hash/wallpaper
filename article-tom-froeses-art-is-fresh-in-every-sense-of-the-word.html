Tom Froese’s Art is Fresh in Every Sense of the Word

By Dan J - August 14, 2019

Tom Froese's artistic practice is characterized by work that is fresh in every sense—visually vibrant, conceptually innovative, and often created using fresh or living materials that add temporal and environmental dimensions to his practice. His approach to art-making frequently incorporates organic materials including plants, flowers, fruits, and vegetables, creating works that celebrate the inherent beauty and visual interest of these living or recently-living materials. The use of fresh materials introduces an element of impermanence and decay into his work, as the materials age and deteriorate, changing the visual character and conceptual significance of the pieces over time. Froese's compositions often involve careful arrangements of fresh materials into patterns, shapes, or forms that create visual interest while remaining recognizable as particular plants or foods.

The color palettes of his work are often vibrant and distinctive, with the natural colors of fruits, flowers, and vegetables providing a rich and varied visual language. His practice engages with contemporary concerns about sustainability, consumption, and the environment, with choices of materials and concepts frequently reflecting these concerns. The work is typically documented through photography before the materials deteriorate, creating a permanent record of the temporarily existing artwork. Froese's approach to artistic practice emphasizes process, materiality, and the poetic possibilities of working with fresh, living, or recently-living materials.

His work has been exhibited internationally and has achieved particular recognition for the innovative and poetic approach to material and concept. Through his fresh, innovative art, Tom Froese has created a distinctive practice that celebrates natural materials and explores the possibilities of ephemeral artistic creation.
