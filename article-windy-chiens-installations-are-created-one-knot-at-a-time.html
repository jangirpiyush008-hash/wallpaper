Windy Chien’s Installations Are Created One Knot At a Time

By Dan J - July 2, 2019

Windy Chien creates large-scale installations and artworks created one knot at a time through the labor-intensive practice of knotting string or cord into elaborate three-dimensional structures and forms. The installations are typically monumental in scale, with individual knots multiplied across vast installations to create complete environments and complex visual experiences. The process of creating these installations involves repetitive, meditative labor, with each knot representing hours of committed artistic work. The resulting installations are visually striking and often occupy substantial architectural and spatial contexts.

The color choices in the installations are often distinctive, with selections of cord colors creating visual patterns and effects across the monumental forms. The installations frequently respond to and engage with specific architectural and spatial contexts, with the knotted forms interacting with and transforming the spaces they occupy. The work engages with themes of labor, meditation, and the philosophical and spiritual dimensions of repetitive, meditative practice. The monumental scale achieved through accumulation of individual knots creates powerful visual experiences that reward sustained contemplation.

Through installations created one knot at a time, Windy Chien has created work that celebrates the power of accumulated labor and the spiritual and aesthetic possibilities of repetitive practice.
