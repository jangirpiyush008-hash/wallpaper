Amy Blackwell’s Paintings Begin With a Color Story

By Rose K - August 29, 2019

Amy Blackwell is a contemporary painter whose artistic practice is organized around color relationships and color theory, with each painting beginning as an exploration of specific color combinations and the emotional and conceptual resonances these colors evoke. Her approach places color at the forefront of artistic concern, often resulting in works that are organized around narrative color stories rather than representational subject matter. Blackwell's process typically begins with the selection of a specific color palette, chosen based on intuition, emotional response, or conceptual considerations relevant to the work being created. From this foundation, she develops the composition and subject matter of the painting, allowing the color story to guide and inform the visual elements that emerge on the canvas.

Her paintings often feature abstract or semi-abstract compositions punctuated by recognizable elements, creating visual experiences that operate on multiple levels of perception and interpretation. The interplay between warm and cool tones, between complementary and analogous colors, creates visual tension and coherence that drives the viewer's experience of the work. Her color choices often appear unexpected or unconventional, creating a sense of discovery and delight as viewers engage with the work and allow their perception to be guided by the color relationships she's established. Her paintings have been exhibited in galleries and museums internationally, with critics particularly praising her sophisticated understanding of color and her ability to use color as a primary means of artistic expression.

Through her color-driven approach to painting, Blackwell has demonstrated that color itself can serve as a primary subject and vehicle for artistic meaning-making, worthy of the same serious artistic consideration traditionally afforded to form, composition, and subject matter.
