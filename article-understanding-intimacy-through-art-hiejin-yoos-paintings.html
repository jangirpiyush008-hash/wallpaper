Understanding Intimacy Through Art: Hiejin Yoo’s Paintings

By Rose K - April 12, 2021

Time-lapse video documentation of artistic processes has emerged as compelling contemporary medium revealing creative work invisible during real-time observation. A particularly remarkable project involved creating three-dimensional sculpture through systematically stacking two thousand carefully cut paper or material slices into a complete form. The time-lapse video compressing hours or days of meticulous labor into minutes of screen time proves simultaneously fascinating and deeply satisfying to watch. The visual transformation from disorganized stacks of slices into coherent sculptural form demonstrates artistic vision's power while celebrating the accumulated labor and precision required for completion.

The sculpture creation process through sequential stacking represents sophisticated artistic concept employing additive methodology creating three-dimensional forms. Rather than removing material as traditional sculpture demands, this additive approach builds forms through controlled accumulation. Each individual slice contains information regarding form's three-dimensional structure—the shape and positioning of individual slices determine overall form's appearance. The conceptual approach combines mathematical precision with artistic vision, requiring both technical calculation and aesthetic judgment.

The project engages with fundamental questions regarding how three-dimensional forms materialize from two-dimensional components. The process reveals relationships between form and its constituent parts, investigating how complex wholes emerge from simple elements. This conceptual engagement elevates the project beyond technical achievement into territory of artistic and philosophical investigation. The resulting sculpture embodies these conceptual concerns through physical form.

Creating convincing three-dimensional form through stacking slices demands exceptional technical precision and careful planning. The artist must determine appropriate slice thickness, material properties, and cutting patterns enabling forms to cohere structurally while achieving desired aesthetic. Computer-aided design facilitates planning, enabling calculation of appropriate slice shapes and positions creating target form. Modern digital tools enable designers to specify complex forms then generate patterns for cutting slices.

The physical execution requires meticulous cutting ensuring each slice matches specifications exactly. Variations in cutting precision accumulate across two thousand slices, potentially creating significant final form distortions. The repetitive nature of cutting demands sustained focus and quality control across extended production period. The technical challenge tests both individual skill and patience, as thousands of cuts must be completed accurately. The accumulated precision throughout thousands of components ensures final form accuracy.

Different materials serve the stacking sculpture process with varying degrees of effectiveness. Paper or cardboard provides economical material enabling quick cutting while creating sufficiently lightweight final forms. The material's rigidity ensures slices maintain shape during stacking while remaining workable for cutting. Other materials including plastic, wood, or acrylic offer different aesthetic and structural properties. The material choice significantly affects sculpture's appearance, weight, and durability—decisions reflecting artistic intentions and practical constraints.

Material color influences visual impact significantly. Monochromatic materials create sculptural form through dimensional shading and light reflection. Colored materials enable more explicit visual differentiation of slices, making stacking process more visible in time-lapse documentation. Some artists deliberately color code slices revealing internal form structure. The material and color choices constitute essential artistic decisions affecting finished sculpture's aesthetic impact.

Documenting the stacking process through time-lapse creates visually compelling transformation sequence wherein unrelated stacks progressively cohere into recognizable form. The compression of extended labor into brief video satisfies viewers' desire for rapid visual resolution while celebrating accumulated effort. The time-lapse format emphasizes transformation—the shift from potential to actuality, from components to complete form. The visual satisfaction viewers experience watching forms materialize reflects deep human appreciation for process transparency and order emerging from apparent chaos.

The time-lapse documentation functions as artwork itself alongside the physical sculpture. The video reveals artistic process typically invisible, celebrating labor and technical precision usually unappreciated by final viewers. The documentation democratizes understanding of artwork creation, showing audiences how remarkable forms emerge from systematic accumulation. The educational dimension encourages viewers to appreciate artistic process and technical accomplishment underlying impressive final products.

The sheer repetition inherent in cutting two thousand slices emphasizes human labor's value and the accumulated significance of small actions. While individual slices appear insignificant, the accumulation of thousands produces remarkable results. The project celebrates handwork and precision manufacturing, demonstrating value in careful execution of repetitive tasks. In contemporary contexts often devaluing manual labor and celebrating only conceptual innovation, the project asserts hands-on work's importance.

The precision required throughout the project connects to craft traditions valuing meticulous execution and material mastery. Though contemporary digital tools assist planning, actual cutting and stacking demands human skill and sustained attention. The combination of digital assistance and manual execution exemplifies contemporary hybrid approaches blending technology with traditional craft. The visible evidence of human labor throughout process grounds artwork in embodied human effort.

The project engages necessarily with mathematical relationships determining how slice shapes combine creating desired three-dimensional form. The process essentially involves discretizing continuous three-dimensional space into manageable two-dimensional slices. Calculating appropriate slice dimensions, positioning, and ordering requires mathematical understanding. Computer algorithms facilitate these calculations, analyzing three-dimensional models and generating two-dimensional slice patterns.

The mathematical dimension reveals relationships between form and geometry often invisible in final artwork. Viewers witnessing transformation in time-lapse might not consciously recognize underlying mathematical principles yet intuitively appreciate the systematic logic ordering slice accumulation. The mathematical precision ensures that process follows intelligible patterns rather than appearing random or chaotic. The order and logic underlying the visible transformation creates satisfaction—viewers perceive meaningful structure rather than arbitrary action.

The two thousand slice scale creates substantial final form—typically large enough to occupy significant gallery or public space. The scale amplifies visual impact, creating immersive artworks commanding attention and inviting extended contemplation. Viewers circulating around completed sculpture examine forms from multiple angles, discovering varied perspectives. The dimensional presence proves more impactful than photographs or representations could convey—the physicality of accumulated material creates authority and presence.

The massive scale heightens appreciation for accumulated labor. Two thousand individual actions accumulate into singular unified form—the scale itself celebrates the significance of human effort and attention distributed across extended duration. The visible traces of cutting and stacking remain legible in completed form, acknowledging process's visibility and importance.

The slice-stacking approach references historical sculpture traditions including wood carving and stone sculpting, wherein forms emerge through material reduction. The additive approach creates interesting contrast with subtractive traditions, demonstrating alternative methodologies achieving similar forms through opposite processes. Contemporary variations on traditional approaches create dialogues between artistic past and present innovation. The project maintains connection with sculpture's historical traditions while employing contemporary materials and techniques.

Completed sculptures require thoughtful installation and display enabling full appreciation of form and materiality. Pedestals, wall mounting, or floor placement affect how viewers experience works. Lighting proves particularly important—directional light reveals dimensional form through shadow and highlight, while diffuse light emphasizes overall composition. Installation designers work carefully to optimize viewer experience and artwork presentation. The display context significantly influences aesthetic impact and meaning communication.

Viewers encountering both physical sculpture and time-lapse documentation experience artwork on multiple levels. The tangible encounter with completed form communicates through scale, materiality, and dimensionality. The time-lapse documentation reveals process transparency and celebrates accumulated labor. The combination of object and documentation creates more complete artistic experience than either alone provides. The satisfaction viewers report watching time-lapse transformation reflects psychological appreciation for rapid order emergence and process visibility.

The fundamental concept of slice-stacking applies to numerous artistic visions and sculptural forms. Artists have employed the approach creating representational sculpture, abstract forms, landscape representations, and purely geometric compositions. Each variation demonstrates adaptability of fundamental methodology while creating distinctly different artworks. The flexibility enables artists to pursue diverse aesthetic and conceptual goals within core technical framework.

Contemporary practitioners continue innovating within the field, employing new materials and developing novel cutting and stacking approaches. Some projects incorporate multiple material types, employing different materials for distinct sculptural components. Others employ mixed-media approaches combining slices with additional sculptural elements. The ongoing development suggests the approach's continued vitality and creative potential.

The time-lapse documentation exemplifies broader contemporary culture emphasizing process visibility and creative documentation. Artists increasingly share work-in-progress images, studio photographs, and process videos developing audiences for their creative practice. The documentation celebrates labor while providing educational value regarding artistic production. The visibility of creative process contrasts with earlier art cultures emphasizing finished products without process transparency.

The emergence of process-focused documentation reflects changing relationships between artists and audiences. Rather than merely presenting completed artworks, contemporary practice invites audiences into creative process itself. This transparency democratizes art appreciation while celebrating artistic labor. The satisfaction viewers report watching creative transformation demonstrates deep human appreciation for witnessing order emerge from complex processes.

The two thousand slice sculpture project exemplifies how contemporary artists employ systematic approaches creating remarkable transformations meriting sustained attention. The combination of technical precision, mathematical calculation, and artistic vision produces artworks simultaneously satisfying aesthetically and conceptually engaging. The time-lapse documentation captures this transformation, creating visual experience celebrating human labor, artistic vision, and accumulated precision. The project demonstrates artistic possibilities within constraint-based, systematic approaches wherein remarkable complexity emerges from disciplined execution of simple repeated actions.
