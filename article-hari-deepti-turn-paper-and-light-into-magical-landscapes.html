Hari & Deepti Turn Paper and Light Into Magical Landscapes

By Rose K - April 23, 2020

Acrylic painting represents one of the most versatile, technically flexible, and distinctively modern painting media, offering contemporary artists unprecedented freedom in technique, application, and conceptual approach. Developed in the mid-twentieth century as synthetic alternative to traditional oil painting, acrylics combine accessibility and rapid drying with capacity for achieving effects from transparent glazes to thick impasto applications. The flexibility of acrylic media has attracted diverse artists working in styles ranging from meticulous realism to gestural abstraction, from flat graphic application to sculptural dimensional work. The rapid adoption of acrylic painting by leading contemporary artists reflects the medium's capacity to support virtually any artistic vision while offering practical advantages over traditional media. Acrylic painting has become dominant medium in contemporary art practice, utilized by institutional galleries, commercial artists, and experimental practitioners.

The chemical composition of acrylic paint involves pigment suspended in water-based polymer emulsion, creating paint with fundamentally different properties than oil paint. The water solubility of wet acrylic permits easy cleaning and rapid application without solvents, reducing toxicity and environmental impact compared to oil painting. The rapid drying of acrylic creates immediate permanence, permitting rapid layering and revision without waiting extended periods between applications. However, the rapid drying also presents challenges regarding working time and requires different technical approaches than oil painting's slower drying characteristics. Contemporary acrylic painters develop sophisticated techniques for managing working time, often employing retarders and stay-wet palettes to extend drying time and permit more deliberate working processes.

The opacity and coverage of acrylic paint differs significantly from oil paint, with acrylics offering exceptional covering power even in thin applications. Acrylic's opacity combined with capacity for thinning with water creates possibilities for both impasto work and transparent glazing effects. The medium permits mixing on canvas, layering transparent applications over opaque grounds, and employing scraffito and other techniques that modify applied paint. This technical flexibility permits individual artists to develop distinctive technical approaches suited to their artistic visions. Contemporary acrylic painters routinely combine multiple techniques within single works, exploiting the medium's capacity for diverse visual effects.
